## Campaign Overview

Juno is running a LinkedIn outreach campaign to find its first design partners and paying customers. The goal is to connect with Heads of Marketing, VP Marketing, Demand Gen leaders, and Performance Marketing managers at B2B SaaS companies in the SF Bay Area who spend $3K-$50K/month on Google Ads and use HubSpot as their CRM.

The campaign launched on April 27, 2026. As of April 28, 2026:
- **16 connection requests sent**
- **0 replies received**
- **0 rejections**
- **97 qualified companies identified** from PitchBook for the next wave

The outreach strategy evolved during the first two days from a direct pitch approach ("I built this, thought it could help") to a two-mode strategy combining coffee/learning conversations with free Google Ads audits.

## GTM Strategy

### Two-Mode Approach (validated April 28, 2026)

**Mode 1: Coffee/Learning**
- For SF-based leads
- Mention Juno as context, not as a pitch
- Offer to buy coffee
- Learn about their day-to-day and how they measure ad ROI
- Genuine curiosity, not a disguised demo
- Goal: build relationship, understand their workflow, find pain points organically

**Mode 2: Free Audit**
- After building rapport (coffee or reply)
- Offer to pull their Google Ads data and show them waste for free
- "Let me find $2K+ of wasted spend in 30 minutes"
- The audit IS the product demo
- No ask to connect their CRM to an unknown tool

### Sequence
1. Connection request (learning tone)
2. Coffee meeting (if SF-based) or follow-up message
3. Free audit offer
4. Design partner conversion

### Volume Target
- 30-50 quality outreaches per week (10 is not enough for signal)
- Multi-channel: LinkedIn + cold email for non-SF leads (Apollo for work emails)
- Content: Post 1-2x/week on LinkedIn showing real (anonymized) Google Ads waste data

### Key Differentiator vs HubSpot
"HubSpot shows you which campaign generated a contact. We show you which keyword generated a deal."

## Outreach Modes

### Mode A: Direct Pitch (used for first 8 contacts)
Simple, straightforward: "I'm building X, thought it could help, let me know."
- Used for: Samantha (Vitally), Gage (Pylon), Ignacio (Maxico), Kamil (Vanta), Henry (Gem), Danielle (Sendoso), Gabriella (Jasper)

### Mode B: Coffee/Learning (adopted April 28)
Softer, relationship-first: "I'm building in this space and want to learn from you."
- Used for: Ryan (Default), Eduardo (Sardine)
- Best for: SF-based leads, senior titles, companies where you want a genuine relationship

Pure connection, zero business angle: "Fellow Kelley alum. Would be great to connect."
- Used for: Arik (AiDASH), Daniel (Supplier.io)
- Best for: Shared alma mater (IU Kelley School). Let the LinkedIn profile (Founder @ Juno + ex-Jefferies) do the selling. Juno angle comes in message 2 if they reply.

Lead with industry-specific pain: "Long enterprise sales cycles kill UTM attribution."
- Used for: Larry (SentiLink), Olivia (Secureframe)
- Best for: Financial services, compliance SaaS, companies with long B2B sales cycles

## Message Rules and Brand Voice

### Hard Rules (Every Message)
1. Under 280 characters for LinkedIn connection request
2. No meeting ask in first message (belongs in message 2 after a reply)
3. No pitch in first message (not even a soft one)
4. No em dash. Use commas or periods instead.
5. Every sentence under 30 words
6. One question maximum per message
7. Peer-to-peer tone throughout
8. No flattery, no bragging

### Never Say
- "I came across your profile"
- "caught my eye"
- "I'd love to connect" (filler)
- "incredible work" / "huge fan" / "love what you've built"
- "Open to a quick chat?" / "15-minute call?" (in first message)
- "We help companies like yours..."
- Never start a message with a verb

### Preferred Phrases
- "Curious if..."
- "Quick question"
- "No [ask], just..."
- "Walk into your next [meeting/board/exec review] knowing..."
- "Zero pipeline"
- "Which keywords are actually turning into pipeline"

### Numbers Rule
Lead with a specific, plausible number whenever possible. Numbers stop the scroll.
- Juno anchor stat: "20-30% of spend going to keywords with zero pipeline"
- Time-bound claims: "cut 20% of your ad spend in 30 minutes"
- Never fabricate a metric specific to the prospect

### Bilingual Rule
If a prospect is Spanish-speaking, write a Spanish variant. Apply all rules identically in both languages.

## Champion Theory

**Core rule: Do NOT target the CEO.**

Target the middle manager who feels the pain most directly: Head of Marketing, VP Marketing, Director of Marketing, Growth Lead. Give them something that makes them look good internally. They become the internal champion and drive the sale upward.

**Frame:** "You'd walk into your next exec meeting knowing which keywords generated pipeline and which ones to cut." The credit goes to the champion.

Manager-level titles (Growth Marketing Manager, Sr Growth Marketing Manager, Marketing Programs Manager) are acceptable as tactical champions at companies under ~200 employees. They often run Google Ads directly and feel the pain most acutely.

| Tier | Titles | Priority | Notes |
|------|--------|----------|-------|
| Tier 1 | Performance Marketer, PPC Manager | Highest | Lives in Google Ads daily. Feels the pain most acutely. |
| Tier 2 | Head of Demand Gen, Senior Demand Gen Manager | High | Owns pipeline metrics. Reports to VP/CMO. |
| Tier 3 | Marketing Ops Manager, GTM Engineer | Medium | Technical evaluator. Lives in the data plumbing. |
| Tier 4 | VP Marketing, CMO | Lower | Decision maker but less likely to accept cold outreach. Better reached through a Tier 1-3 champion. |

## Lessons Learned

### What Worked
1. **Vertical-specific angles** performed best in draft quality (SentiLink finance angle, Secureframe compliance angle). These messages reference pain the prospect actually feels.
2. **Coffee/learning mode** feels more authentic for SF-based leads and avoids the "cold pitch from stranger" dynamic.
3. **Spanish variants** for bilingual prospects (Ignacio, Eduardo) add a personal touch that's hard to replicate at scale.
4. **Alumni connections** (Kelley/IU) provide a warm entry point with zero business pressure.
5. **Numbers as market observation** (council-validated Apr 29): "20-30% of spend going to keywords with zero pipeline" framed as an industry finding, not a promise. Avoid outcome claims ("we save 20%") and accusatory framing ("your team is wasting 20%"). Numbers stop the scroll when framed as peer-to-peer insight.
6. **Apollo AI search** worked well for batch lead discovery — 39 results from Tier 1 companies in one prompt.

### What Didn't Work / Changed
1. **Direct pitch in first message** was the initial approach but shifted to coffee/learning after the user realized relationship-building is more sustainable.
2. **Product Marketing titles** (e.g., Yoav Shilon at Drata) were flagged as skip. Product Marketing doesn't own Google Ads or attribution.
3. **10 outreaches per week** is not enough for signal. Raised target to 30-50.
4. **InMail as first touch** was rejected. Connection request feels peer-level. InMail is for follow-up only.

### Strategic Decisions
1. The **free audit** is the product demo. Don't ask prospects to connect their CRM to an unknown tool. Pull their Google Ads data yourself and show them the waste.
2. **Send as connection request**, not InMail. VP/Head-level gets too many InMails from vendors.
3. **Message 1 = conversation starter.** Message 2 = coffee or audit offer. Message 3 = specific value. Never compress the sequence.
4. **Multi-channel after LinkedIn:** If no reply in 7 days, follow up via cold email (Apollo for work emails).

## Follow-Up Protocol

### If Connection Accepted (no message reply)
Wait 1-2 days, then send a short follow-up introducing the free audit offer:
"Thanks for connecting, [Name]. I've been auditing Google Ads accounts for B2B teams and finding 20-30% of spend going to keywords with zero pipeline. Happy to pull that for [Company] — takes about 30 minutes, no strings. Interested?"

### If They Reply Positively
Move to coffee (SF) or free audit (non-SF):
- SF: "Would love to grab coffee and walk through it in person. I'm in [neighborhood]. What works for you this week?"
- Non-SF: "Happy to pull the data and share what I find. Would a 20-minute call work to walk through it?"

### If They Reply Negatively / Not Interested
Exit cleanly: "Totally understand. If you ever want a second set of eyes on your attribution setup, happy to revisit."

### If No Response After 7 Days
Follow up via InMail or cold email (Apollo). Different angle, same rules.
